What are these sources of income?

Savings income

An account that you get interest from, such as:

  • bank and building society accounts
  • accounts with providers like credit unions or National Savings and Investments

It does not include tax-free interest from ISAs.

Investment income

This is usually a ‘chargeable event gains’ from a life insurance policy.

Dividend income

Where you get shares or cash from a UK company that you have invested in.

Pension income

Income from sources where you have reached a certain age, including:

  • Annuity payments from a pension scheme
  • Industrial Death Benefit
  • Widowed Mother’s Allowance
  • Widowed Parent’s Allowance

It does not include the Christmas bonus, winter fuel payment, or war pensions.

Trusts, settlements or estates

You will receive an R185 form with all the necessary information from the trustees or administrators.

Foreign income

Any income you get from outside of England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland (including the Channel Islands and the Isle of Man) such as:

  • wages from working abroad
  • foreign investments and savings interest
  • rental income on an overseas property
  • income from pensions held overseas

Notional income

Income that you are treated as having which you may not in fact have, such as:

  • employment income where you worked for free or for less than the going rate
  • income that you could get but did not apply for such as Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • income you did not take, so you could get tax credits or more tax credits
  • investment income of a child where you have provided trust funds of more than £100

What are these benefits?

Bereavement Allowance

A benefit you can claim if you are widowed between 45 and State Pension age (previously known as Widow’s Pension).

Carer’s Allowance

A benefit you can claim if you care for someone at least 35 hours a week and they are claiming a benefit.

Contribution-based Jobseeker’s Allowance

An unemployment benefit you can claim if you have paid enough National Insurance contributions through an employer in the last 2 tax years and are looking for work.

Contribution-based Employment and Support Allowance

A benefit you can claim if your ability to work is limited by ill health or disability and you have paid enough National Insurance contributions through an employer in the last 2 tax years.

Incapacity Benefit

A benefit you can claim if your ability to work is limited by ill health or disability. It is being replaced with Employment and Support Allowance (ESA).
